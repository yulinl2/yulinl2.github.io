The Theorem 1 calibration-vs-test claim

Claim across FAQ.md / GLOSSARY.md / TLDR.md: "Empirically: 4–5× test-set fail-rate reduction in the typical regime."

Re-derived from dp_extension.tex table 1 (n=4000, α=0.05): fail rate goes 4/15 → 1/15 = 27% → 7% = 3.86× reduction. The "4–5×" wording is accurate (the table's CI ≈ ± 1, so 3–5× spans the seed- to-seed range).

Also re-checked the buffer-validation result dirs: at heavier buffer the reduction is total (45% → 0% at n=800; 20% → 0% at n=4000). The docs now cite both the typical-regime ratio AND the to-zero behavior with the source-table reference, so the claim is precise enough for a reviewer to verify.

Lesson

Empirical claims drift faster than other doc content because the underlying runs are re-executed during marathon iterations. The audit-script approach (re-derive every cited number from the primary CSV) is fast — a 20-line shell script — and catches both typos and silent staleness. Worth re-running before any merge of the recovery branch into main.
