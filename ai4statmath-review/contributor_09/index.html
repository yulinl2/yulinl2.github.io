**Problem.** Ten independent Bernoulli trials have success probabilities 1/2, 1/3,..., 1/11. Find the exact probability of exactly 5 successes. **Approach.** The trials are indexed by $k = 2, 3, \dots, 11$, with success probability $p_k = 1/k$ and failure probability $q_k = 1 - 1/k = (k-1)/k$. This is a Poisson-binomial distribution. The probability generating function for the number of successes is $$G(z) = \prod_{k=2}^{11}\big(q_k + p_k z\big) = \prod_{k=2}^{11}\left[\frac{k-1}{k} + \frac{1}{k}z\right].$$ The probability of exactly 5 successes is the coefficient of $z^5$ in $G(z)$. **Computation.** Factor out the common denominator $\prod_{k=2}^{11} k = 11! / 1 = 39916800$... more cleanly, write $$G(z) = \frac{1}{\prod_{k=2}^{11} k}\prod_{k=2}^{11}\big[(k-1) + z\big].$$ Here $\prod_{k=2}^{11} k = 2\cdot3\cdots11 = 11!/1! = 39916800$, and $\prod_{k=2}^{11}[(k-1)+z] = \prod_{j=1}^{10}(j+z) = (z+1)(z+2)\cdots(z+10)$, whose coefficients are the (unsigned) Stirling numbers of the first kind. Then $$ a\cdot 1 + b\cdot 2 = 5,\qquad a,b\ge 0. $$ The solutions are $(a,b) \in \{(5,0),(3,1),(1,2)\}$. **Contribution of each case.** Choosing the degree-1 term from a set $S$ of factors multiplies in $\prod_{j\in S} j$; choosing the degree-2 term contributes a factor of $1$ each. A frame member counts as a nonrespondent to the medication item if they failed to provide that item for any reason — whether because they were never reached (unit nonresponse) or because they were reached but skipped the question (item nonresponse).
