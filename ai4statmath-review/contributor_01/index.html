\begin{answerkey}
To find the smallest positive integer $n$ such that $\frac{1}{n}$ is a terminating decimal, we must first look at the mathematical rule for terminating decimals.

A fraction $\frac{1}{n}$ (in simplest form) will terminate if and only if the prime factorization of its denominator $n$ contains no prime numbers other than $2$ or $5$. Therefore, $n$ must be of the form:
$$n = 2^a \cdot 5^b$$
where $a$ and $b$ are non-negative integers.

We need to find the smallest number of this form that contains the digit 9. Let's generate the candidates in increasing order systematically by checking combinations of $2^a \cdot 5^b$:

\begin{itemize}
    \item 1 to 100: 1, 2, 4, 5, 8, 10, 16, 20, 25, 32, 40, 50, 64, 80, 100, not satisfied.
    \item 101 to 500: 125, 128, 160, 200, 250, 256, 320, 400, 500, not satisfied.
    \item 501 to 1000: 512, 625, 640, 800, 1000, not satisfied.
    \item 1001 to 2000: 1024, 1250, 1280, 1600, 2000, not satisfed.
    \item 2001 to 4000: 2048, 2500, 2560, 3125, 3200, 4000, not satisfied.
\end{itemize}
The very next number in the sequence of numbers generated by $2^a \cdot 5^b$ is $2^{12}$:
$$2^{12} = 4096$$
Because $4096$ contains the digit 9, and $\frac{1}{4096}$ is a terminating decimal (it is exactly $0.000244140625$), the smallest positive integer $n$ that satisfies both conditions is 4096.
\end{answerkey}

# Solution

**Step 1: Find the distance traveled**

Since all 3 kangaroos travel at the same speed and together take 18 hours, each individual kangaroo takes 18 hours to cross the highway.

Let's say each kangaroo travels at speed $v$.
- Distance = $v \times 18$ hours

**Step 2: Find the speed of each turtle**

Each turtle travels at half the speed of a kangaroo:
- Turtle speed = $\frac{v}{2}$

**Step 3: Calculate time for turtles**

Since all 4 turtles travel at the same speed, each turtle takes the same time to cross.

Using distance = speed × time:
$$18v = \frac{v}{2} \times t$$

$$t = \frac{18v}{\frac{v}{2}} = 18v \times \frac{2}{v} = 36 \text{ hours}$$
