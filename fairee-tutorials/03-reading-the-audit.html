3 · Reading the audit

Every audit_status message the runner can emit, what it means about your run, and what to do about it.

Reading time ≈ 7 min · Refers to src/engine/analysis/audit.py.

Anatomy of an audit result

{
  "status": "warn",                        # ok | warn | fail
  "warnings": [                            # FAIL: > WARN: > INFO: prefixed
    "WARN: max IPW weight 12.4 > 10. ...",
    "INFO: self-censoring strength 0.42 > 0.30. ..."
  ],
  "n_min_required": 146                    # sample-size floor for (alpha_eff, delta)
}

Status is the worst-severity prefix found in warnings: FAIL bubbles up to fail, WARN to warn, plain INFO stays at ok.

The 7 checks (in order)

1. FAIL Empirical (test-set) budget compliance

If eoo_gap (or dp_gap when the method targets DP) exceeds α on the test set.

Important nuance: the FaiREE theorem bounds the calibration gap, not the test-set realization. A FAIL here doesn't necessarily mean the algorithm is broken — it can also mean finite-sample noise on the test set pushed the realized gap over budget. Check the cal-gap check (1b) below.

Action: if cal-gap is below α but test-set is over, set generalization_buffer="auto" on retry. The theorem-bounded calibration gap stays valid, and the buffer absorbs the test-set noise (empirically 4–5× reduction in test-set fail rate).

1b. WARN Calibration-set fairness gap above α

If diag.cal_deoo (or cal_dp) exceeds α. This is what the theorem actually bounds; over-budget here means the Beta-quantile admission selected a candidate near the boundary.

Action: tighten α, collect more calibration data, or accept that this regime degenerates to the trivial fallback.

2. WARN Within-group AIPW spread exceeds 0.05 (M2 diagnostic)

If dr_spread > 0.05. This is your empirical signal for Assumption M2 ("no outcome shift across the missingness pattern indicator ξ").

Action: if you're using fairee_nsc or fairee_dp_nsc, the IPW estimator inherits bias; switch to fairee_dr_if_bootstrap (which is robust to M2 violation as long as one nuisance is correctly specified).

3. WARN Max IPW weight exceeds 10 (M3 / weight stability)

If diag.w_max > 10. The slack term in Theorem 1 scales linearly with W; W > 10 makes the bound uninformative.

Action: tighten w_clip in NSCDensityRatio (e.g., 5.0). May trade a small amount of bias for variance.

3a. WARN Bootstrap-B too small (for bootstrap-admission methods)

If diag.admission_test ∈ {bootstrap, if_bootstrap} and diag.bootstrap_B < 200. The uniform-in-B slack σ_ψ/(2·φ(z_{1-δ/2})) · √(log(8/δ)/(2B)) may dominate the α budget.

Action: bump bootstrap_B to ≥ 500 for the headline regime (δ = 0.10).

3b. INFO Bernstein-Var tightening recommended

If diag.w_std / diag.w_max < 0.5 — your weight distribution is concentrated and the Hoeffding-W bound is loose by a factor of w_max / w_std.

Action: compute the Bernstein-Var(w) bound from proof_sketch.tex sec:w1_constants "Tightening to Var(w)" paragraph for a sharper per-run slack.

4. INFO Self-censoring strength exceeds 0.3

If diag.sc_strength = ‖γ̂‖ / ‖α̂‖ > 0.3. Your data require the full Li-self-censoring model (not the Sadinle-NSC sub-case at γ=0).

Action: the current NSCDensityRatio handles this correctly via free γ fit, but convergence may benefit from more BFGS iterations (max_iter=160).

5. WARN Effective cell size below the sample-size floor

If the minimum effective positive-cell size is below ⌈log(δ/4) / log(1 − α_eff/2)⌉. The Beta-test will degenerate to the trivial all-negative predictor.

Action: relax α (e.g., 0.05 → 0.10), collect more cal data, or use the DP variant which has 3–5× larger cells.

Common combinations

What you seeWhat it usually meansWhat to try
FAIL empirical gap + ok cal gap Test-set noise, not methodology failure Add generalization_buffer="auto"
WARN w_max + INFO Bernstein NSC fit produced a heavy-tail weight distribution Tighten w_clip; compute Bernstein-Var slack
WARN sample-size floor under EOO EOO cells too small at strict α Switch to DP variant (larger cells) or relax α
WARN dr_spread > 0.05 M2 (no outcome shift across ξ) potentially violated Use fairee_dr_if_bootstrap (robust to M2)

Calling the audit standalone

from engine.analysis.audit import audit
report = audit(r, alpha=0.10)        # r is a run_once() result dict
print(report["status"])              # 'ok' | 'warn' | 'fail'
for w in report["warnings"]:
    print(w)
